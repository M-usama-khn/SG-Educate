Understanding the Essence of Keyword Research

Keyword research is the backbone of online content. At its core, it’s about understanding what people are searching for and why. Let’s break it down in simpler terms.

Types of Keywords

There are primarily four types of keywords according to search intent:

  • Informational: This is when people are on a quest for knowledge. They might be looking for tutorials, guides, or answers to specific questions. While platforms like YouTube offer video explanations, search engines provide written content to cater to this need.
  • Navigational: It’s a basic search, often leading to a specific website. For instance, someone might type “Apple” intending to visit Apple’s official website. For content creators, this intent isn’t our primary focus. Commercial: Here, users are exploring brands or services. They might not be ready to buy but are gathering information about potential purchases.
  • Transactional: This is the research phase before making a purchase. Instead of directly searching for a product, users might look for comparisons, reviews, or the best options within a certain budget. For instance, “best laptops under $1000” would fall under this category.
